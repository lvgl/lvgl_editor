To start the link between [LVGL Pro Editor](../editor) and LVGL Flow for Figma, first head to the editor and start the service.

## Starting the service

In LVGL Pro Editor, click the **Figma button** in the toolbar.

<Figure
  src="/_static/images/figma/launch-flow.png"
  alt="The Figma button in the LVGL Pro Editor toolbar"
  caption="Open the panel from the Figma button in the LVGL Pro Editor toolbar."
  width={289}
  height={137}
/>

The **LVGL Figma Flow** panel opens. Click **Start Service**. That's the whole setup.

<Figure
  src="/_static/images/figma/editor-connection.png"
  alt="The LVGL Figma Flow control panel with a Start Service button"
  width={500}
  height={500}
  caption="Click Start Service. The service comes up and the plugin connects automatically."
/>

Back in Figma, the plugin connects on its own: the status dot beside your project name
turns green, and it shows the repo, branch, and path it's working in.

<Figure
  src="/_static/images/figma/connection-panel.png"
  alt="The plugin's Connection panel, connected"
  caption="Connected: the plugin shows your project's repo, branch, and path."
/>

<Callout type="success">
  Turn on **Start service when the IDE opens** in the panel's Settings and it's running
  every time the editor launches.
</Callout>

## Settings and ports

Everything the service needs is in that panel:

- **Output folder**: where files get written (defaults to your workspace root).
- **Offline fonts**: resolve fonts locally instead of fetching from Google Fonts.
- **Start service when the IDE opens**: auto-start, so it's always ready.

The service and plugin both default to the **Alpha** port preset, so they match out of the
box, and you won't normally need to touch this. If a port is already in use, switch **both** ends to
**Beta** or **Gamma**, then **Restart Service** (the service reads settings at start).

More in [Troubleshooting](./troubleshooting).


## If it won't connect

Nine times out of ten it just works. If the plugin reads **Can't reach LVGL Pro**, the
[Troubleshooting](./troubleshooting) page has the fix: usually the service isn't started
yet, or the two ends are on different presets.
